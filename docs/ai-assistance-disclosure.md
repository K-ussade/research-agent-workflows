# AI Assistance Disclosure

## Purpose

This document defines public-safe disclosure language for AI-assisted research workflows.

## Disclosure Standard

Disclosures should state:

- whether AI assisted with organization, drafting, summarization, citation formatting, or editorial checklists;
- that human editorial review remains required;
- that AI output is not authoritative by itself;
- that source review and citation checks control public claims;
- that private or sealed material is excluded from public examples.

## Example Disclosure Pattern

```text
AI assistance was used to organize a draft and prepare review checklists. Human editorial review is required before publication. Claims should be checked against cited sources. This draft does not use private research notes, private prompts, private model outputs, donor data, student data, volunteer private data, customer data, private Foundation operations, or sealed YOSO-YAi LLC IP.
```

## Non-Claims

Do not use disclosure language to imply:

- an AI system independently verified the research;
- an autonomous publishing workflow is operating;
- private or sealed material was sanitized by AI alone;
- source review, editorial review, legal review, or publication review has happened when it has not.
