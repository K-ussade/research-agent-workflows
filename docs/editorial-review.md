# Editorial Review

## Purpose

Editorial review ensures that AI-assisted research output is accurate enough for its public-safe purpose, status-labeled, cited, and boundary-compliant.

## Review Gates

| Gate | Trigger | Reviewer Checks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope gate | Research question is accepted. | Purpose, audience, public/private/sealed boundary. |
| Source gate | Sources are selected. | Public availability, relevance, reliability, and copyright handling. |
| Citation gate | Draft claims are written. | Every factual claim ties to a source or is removed. |
| Boundary gate | Draft references organizations, programs, people, infrastructure, or data. | No private or sealed material is exposed. |
| Disclosure gate | AI assistance was used. | Disclosure language is accurate and not overbroad. |
| Publishing gate | Work is ready to publish. | Human approval, status label, links, and exclusions. |

## Editorial Decisions

| Decision | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Approved | Ready for the specified public-safe use. |
| Returned | Needs correction before reuse. |
| Limited | May be used only with stated limitation. |
| Blocked | Cannot be published or reused. |
| Escalated | Requires privacy, security, governance, legal, or publication review. |

## Non-Approval

AI confidence, a clean spellcheck, a complete citation table, or validation success is not editorial approval. Approval must be a human decision for a defined artifact and use.
