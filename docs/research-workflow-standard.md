# Research Workflow Standard

## Purpose

This standard defines a public-safe workflow for AI-assisted research operations.

## Workflow Stages

| Stage | Required Output | Human Gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Question intake | Research question and boundary classification. | Scope review. |
| Source discovery | Candidate source list with public/private status. | Source review. |
| Source review | Reliability, relevance, citation, and licensing notes. | Source approval. |
| Citation logging | Structured citation log. | Citation check. |
| Brief drafting | Draft research brief with claims tied to citations. | Editorial review. |
| Publication preparation | Checklist, disclosure, status label, and boundary review. | Publishing approval. |

## AI Assistance Rules

- AI output is draft assistance and not authoritative without human editorial review.
- AI may organize sources, draft summaries, build citation tables, and identify questions for review.
- AI must not invent citations, fabricate quotes, copy scraped copyrighted content, or convert private notes into public claims.
- AI must not publish, post, send, or update public surfaces without human approval.

## Refusal Triggers

Stop and escalate when a task asks for private research notes, private prompts, private model outputs, donor data, student data, volunteer private data, customer data, private Foundation operations, credentials, secrets, private training corpora, exact sensitive infrastructure locations, security-sensitive NEURONA operational details, sealed YOSO-YAi LLC IP, or unreviewed claims.
